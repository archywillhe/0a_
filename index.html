Zeroth Arch-IO (or 0A.IO in short) is an upcoming decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) for developing open-source frameworks and simulations with the aim to

1. provide production-ready software systems and consultation services to governments around the world to utilize Large Language Models for administrative automation, integrated into a blockchain system to enhance accountability and transparency.

2. push for the research and development of neurons-in-a-dish-driven AI humanoids, insectoids to advance medicines and replace human labour and health care providers for the ever-growing aging populations on Earth.



1. and 2. can go in parallel and will be complimentary to each other. For 2, 0A.IO's belief is that the key will be about designing and implementing a "consciousness orchestration" bio-computing framework that will enable these neurons to learn (mimicking the way biological brain works) and control robotic parts i.e. attaining psychomotor skills like balancing. This is highly likely the only feasible way to create fault tolerant systems with the highest energy-efficiency (as a consequence of the free energy principle in Bayesian mechanics). By open-sourcing the softwares and research findings, everyone will be able to grow and farm neurons-in-a-dish in home-labs and combining them with hardwares to custom-build humanoids and insectoids. This will be significantly much more cost-effective than e.g. R&D systems Boston Dynamics had developed.

0A.IO only has a single agenda, and that is to pragmatically reduce unnecessary suffering for all sentient beings on Earth. This is important as humanity develops powerful AIs and better computational theroies of what consciousness is. It is only through mindfulness and the collective readiness to act and improve the existing status quo can we enhance the overall well-being of Earth's inhabitants, and cultivate a mutually beneficial coexistence.

Taking inspiration from Buddhism, a fundamental belief of 0A.IO is that reducing suffering and exploring the structural complexity of consciousness will lead to novel solutions for hard problems in science and engineering e.g. from an emergentist perspecitve. This will also allow us to create a more connected and empathetic society that values symbiotic relationships for mutual growth.

This would also mean departuring from anthropocentrism, in the sense of embracing a heliocentric-equivalent view of consciousness. The same way that the Galilean depaturing from geocentricism in the 1600-1800 had helped to pave the way for the modern era of scientific thought and discovery, none-anthropocentrism will free our languages and conceptualization of the world around us from self-imposed constraints. Moreover, anthropocentrism as an attitude often encourages self-entitlement across all cultures, fosters tribalism (e.g. "some humans are more equal than others"), justifies exploitation, and constantly undermines the sentience of other life forms (which often hold invaluable insights about the nature of being, optimization, and problem-solving). All of humanity's hard problems can only be solved if we are more mindful, collectively, and of progressing as a global society, taking accountability as a species and as individuals.
