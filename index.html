Zeroth Arch-IO (or 0A.IO in short) is an upcoming decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) for developing open-source frameworks and simulations with the aim to

1. provide production-ready software systems and consultation services to governments around the world to utilize Large Language Models for administrative automation, integrated into a blockchain system to enhance accountability and transparency.

2. push for the research and development of neurons-in-a-dish-driven AI humanoids and insectoids to replace human soliders.



0A.IO only has a single agenda, and that is to pragmatically reduce unnecessary suffering for all sentient beings on Earth. This is important as humanity develops powerful AIs and better computational theroies of what consciousness is. It is only through mindfulness and the collective readiness to act and improve the existing status quo can we enhance the overall well-being of Earth's inhabitants, and cultivate a mutually beneficial coexistence.

Taking inspiration from Buddhism, the fundamental belief of 0A.IO is that reducing suffering and exploring the structural complexity of consciousness will lead to novel solutions for hard problems in science and engineering e.g. from an emergentist perspecitve, as well as allowing us to create a more connected and empathetic society that respects all forms of life and values symbiotic relationships for mutual growth.

This means a departure from anthropocentrism. Anthropocentrism encourages self-entitlement across all cultures, fosters tribalism (e.g. "some humans are more equal than others"), justifies exploitation, and undermines the sentience of other life forms (which often hold invaluable insights about the nature of being, optimization, and problem-solving). All of humanity's hard problems can only be solved if we are more mindful of progressing as a global society, and taking accountability as individuals, and as a species.

[etymology of 0A.IO (Zeroth Arch-IO)]

Zeroth adjective
preceding what is regarded as first.

Arch- prefix
denoting individuals or institutions directing or having authority over others of their class. (e.g. archetype)

IO noun
input/output, i.e. the communication between an information processing system and the outside world.
